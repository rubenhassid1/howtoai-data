It’s a simple indicator, with a high probability taste. What does that mean? It says the most likely thing, very well. But that does not mean it is the truth.

## 2. How to challenge ChatGPT (to avoid bias).

It’s hard to know when to challenge / not challenge ChatGPT.

How could you know it’s the moment you should trust it, or pivot?

Well, taste & experience! 

That’s the difference between a good human using AI, and an average one.

Now I do have a trick to try to have a better **gut feeling**.

It’s a prompting technique called “**Perspective Transition**”. 

I prompt ChatGPT (as a follow-up) with something along these lines:

> *I want to make sure we are not both falling into some confirmation bias.**You will now enact two separate experts (you must choose) that will have a drastically different view on the matter we just discussed.**Both will share their point of view, with sources, quotes & rationales.**Enact one expert, then draw a line break and enact the other one.*
[
![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1RYI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F190bb6fb-39ba-48ff-b3aa-74a75c6d7eb3_966x1930.png)
