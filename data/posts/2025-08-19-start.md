## Step 1. How to prompt?

ChatGPT is the only AI for 99.9% of the population.

And ChatGPT is just as good as its prompt (= what you write).

Let’s go over the basics of prompt engineering, or the art of giving the correct instructions to ChatGPT to get what you want from it.

### What ChatGPT is (and what it isn’t)

- It’s a language model: it predicts the next best words based on your instructions and context. Think “SMS auto correct” on steroids.- It isn’t a fact database or a guaranteed source of truth. It can be wrong or outdated if you don’t enable web lookups.- It follows constraints you give it. Vague inputs = vague outputs.- Treat it like a junior analyst with infinite energy: you set the brief, quality bar, and checks.

### Pick the right model (default: Thinking)

- Use GPT-5 Thinking for analysis, multi-step reasoning, editing, and structured work (almost always).- Switch to a faster non-thinking model for quick answers (almost never).- Turn on search with /search to make sure it searches the web.- Deep Research takes forever, but it is the best feature of ChatGPT to search the web like crazy. Use it.

### Prompt framework

Template you can reuse:

- Role: “Act as a [job]”- Task: “Do [one clear job]”- Context: “Audience, goal, tone, inputs”- Example (optional but powerful): “Here’s a model example…”- Constraints: “Word count, format, do/don’t”- Output spec: “Return [structure]. No preamble.”- QA: “List assumptions, ask two clarifying questions if needed.”

### “No example” vs. “one example”

- You (almost) always want to give an example to ChatGPT of what you want to achieve in your prompt. It’s called “one-shot prompting” (vs. zero-shot, which is to give zero examples).- No example: Best when the task is simple and conventions are obvious (e.g., summarize, bullet key takeaways).- One clear example: Best when you care about style, structure, or voice. Provide a short “golden” sample and say, “match this.”- Rule of thumb: if the output must look/feel a specific way, include one model example.

### Build your own GPTs (reusable instructions)

Building GPTs is awesome. I use it all of the time. Basically, it’s a “pre-trained” ChatGPT to do specific tasks the way you trained it. And you don’t have to copy & paste prompts to get there. You do it once.

Here’s how:

### Use Projects (working memory for real work)

- You can create “Projects” inside ChatGPT. It segments all of these chats for one specific Project. So the memory/context is strictly for this. Super useful.- When: multi-asset research, ongoing threads, or team workflows.- How to structure:Create a Project with a single-sentence objective.- Add sources: briefs, datasets, past posts, client voice docs.- Pin standards: tone guide, formatting rules, citation spec.

### Common pitfalls → quick fixes

- Pitfall: 3 jobs in one prompt. Fix: split into sequential steps.- Pitfall: “Make it better.” Fix: define “better” (metrics, style, length).- Pitfall: Style drift. Fix: include your writing and say “match my style”.- Pitfall: Bloated outputs. Fix: start with the outline. Follow up with more chats (instead of trying to get everything from one prompt & one answer).
