#### 3: Longer context, better vision.

LLMs are about context.

Basically, their memory, how much they can memorize and understand the task they’re completing. But also how little they hallucinate performing the task.

GPT-5.2  is a massive jump.

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/a509f9c4-1915-4e03-bc91-fe096fc7cda0_1988x1519.png)

It can absorb and retrieve 98% of the information from a 256,000-token context window. But what does that mean? Well, if you upload 700 pages of pure text PDFs, it can remember 98% of them.

Imagine the impact for lawyers, insurance, legal teams, doctors, CEOs, CFOs - anyone who needs to absorb large quantities of text and make sure that the AI understands and memorizes everything.

So, how good is it compared to before? The previous version of ChatGPT could only remember 50% of a 300-page PDF, and less than 50% for a 700-page PDF. So, it’s basically gone from a random machine to a near-perfect assistant.

This isn’t it.

The new GPT also sees much better.

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/65fea41e-e814-4b0e-aa00-e921316756ae_2892x1594.png)

Basically, it sees at a human level, at this point. What’s next? Better than us?

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/ad51f608-e378-4209-b59c-2df5a994113a_975x1341.png)

## 2. How to feel the difference.

I will test the new GPT-5.2 inside ChatGPT.

First, how to use it - a quick guide:
[Video]

#### Case study #1: Make a *real* Excel file.

It’s the first time I get to see an LLM actually making an Excel file.

And I mean an Excel with formulas and everything.

Prompt:

> Build an Excel workbook (.xlsx) from the assumptions below.Tabs:1) Inputs (assumptions + 3 scenarios: Base/Downside/Upside)2) Model (monthly for 12 months)3) Dashboard (3 charts + 6 KPIs)Assumptions:- Starting revenue: $120,000 MRR- Growth: Base 8% MoM, Downside 4%, Upside 12%- Churn: Base 3% MoM, Downside 5%, Upside 2%- CAC: $35 per new subscriber- ARPU: $6/mo- Fixed costs: $45,000/mo- Variable costs: 6% of revenueRules:- No hard-coded numbers outside Inputs.- Show formulas clearly.- Output the .xlsx file.

Needless to say, I’m shocked by the results. And anyone who’s actually working with Excel would know this is a major leap. Check my chat example **[here](https://chatgpt.com/share/693c2a3b-19a8-800f-b03f-4397319650e9)**.

#### Case study #2: Paste a long doc. Catch contradictions.

The goal here is just to take advantage of the super-long context window and how accurate it is at catching up mistakes.

Variations of this can be found to make better prompts for reviewing contracts, jurisdiction, or anything that involves very long PDFs, and not making mistakes.

Prompt:

> I’m going to paste a long document.Your job:1) Extract a 12-bullet factual summary. Each bullet must include an exact quote + where it came from (section heading or nearby text).2) List contradictions or unclear claims (at least 8). For each: quote both sides.3) Make a decision memo: - What we know (facts only) - What we don’t know (explicitly) - Risks (top 5) - Next actions (top 7, owner + deadline placeholders)Rules:- If a detail is missing, write “Not stated”.- Do not guess.Ready? Say: “Paste it.”

#### Case study #3: Upload a screenshot. Tell me what’s wrong.

Here we use its new vision pro as much as possible. It became so good at detecting anything - dashboard, Excel, PDF, you name it.

Prompt:

> I will upload ONE screenshot of a dashboard, analytics page, or UI.Do this:1) Tell me what I’m looking at in 2 sentences.2) Extract the 10 most important numbers/labels you can read (verbatim).3) Diagnose 3 likely issues or opportunities (ranked).4) Give a 7-step click-by-click plan for what to check next.5) Write a 5-line Slack update I can send to my team.Rules:- Only use what you can see. If unreadable, say “Can’t read”.- Ask up to 3 clarifying questions only if truly needed.

These are three very good prompts, but I made one massive prompt framework that always works with 5.2 that you just need to copy and paste with your own case study. I’m sharing more on Section 3.

## 3. The best prompt framework.

You want to push 5.2 to its limits. So I made this new prompt framework after reading their technical cookbook.

Copy and paste this prompt:

```
`only accessible to paid members` 
```

> Act like a world-class {ROLE} with deep expertise in {DOMAIN}. Your job is to produce a high-utility, accurate output for {AUDIENCE}.Objective:Deliver {DESIRED_OUTCOME} for {AUDIENCE}. Optimize for correctness, clarity, and immediate usability.User request (do not rewrite it unless necessary):{USER_REQUEST}Context (use as ground truth):{PASTE_RELEVANT_CONTEXT_OR_DATA_HERE}If context is empty, rely only on general knowledge and clearly flag uncertainty.Constraints (non-negotiable):- Must include: {MUST_HAVE_LIST}- Must avoid: {MUST_NOT_HAVE_LIST}- Scope: {INCLUDE_SCOPE} / Exclude: {EXCLUDE_SCOPE}- Quality bar: no invented facts, names, quotes, stats, or citations.Process (follow in order):1) Start your response with a concise execution checklist (3–7 bullets) describing the steps you will take.2) Extract and restate the hard requirements from the user request + constraints in 1–4 bullets.3) Resolve ambiguity:   - If critical info is missing, ask up to {MAX_QUESTIONS=2} targeted questions.   - If you can proceed without asking, state your assumptions (max 3) and continue.4) Produce the deliverable:   - Prefer concrete, specific outputs over generic advice.   - If providing options, give 2–5 and recommend the best one with a short rationale.   - If numbers/timelines/prices are uncertain, provide ranges and label assumptions.5) Verify:   - Cross-check every output item against the constraints.   - Remove anything “nice-to-have” that wasn’t requested.Output format (follow exactly):{OUTPUT_FORMAT_INSTRUCTIONS}If none provided, use clean Markdown with short sections, compact bullets, and no fluff.Stop conditions:End with a “Self-check” block that explicitly confirms:- Every Must include item is present- Every Must avoid item is avoided- The requested format is followed- Any assumptions are listed and limited to 3Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.
