## 1. How to instantly optimize your Linkedin.

Imagine I am sitting next to you,  looking over your shoulder at your Linkedin.

I need to give you the best advice for your Linkedin, in  less than 5 minutes.

This is what I would say:

#### A. Your profile picture sucks.

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/f6db0782-b663-412e-872d-6e2b72b1a905_1800x1047.jpeg)

✦ Smile way more than you think.

✦ Own a color. Instantly recognizable.

✦ Zoom until your entire face is the only thing visible.

✦ Don’t make me confuse: declutter the background, hard color works better.

You must make this quick test: go on Linkedin & check your profile picture from a distance (in the comment section). You must see two things: a **smile**, a **color** without a background.

My own profile picture is made by AI (with Gemini & Nano-Banana Pro).

1. You upload a picture of yourself. HD is needed.2. You upload a picture of a profile picture you love.3. You add this simple prompt: “Generate a corporate headshot of my image in [how you are dressed] in the same style as the 2nd image I uploaded as an inspiration”.

#### A. Get support from others. The bigger, the better.

This will be controversial. But I’ll say it anyway.

Yes, my commenting on Maria Zhanette’s account had an impact.

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/f77701dd-46b0-42d3-b83b-704305c80029_1318x1570.png)

100,000+ impressions on a single comment under her post. Almost 100 likes.

It for sure helped this post go viral. And she got a lot of followers from it.

But I call it the social media game theory: **I help you, hoping you will, and if you don’t, I’ll stop. **

The rules are simple:

1. Support people before expecting people to support you.2. Don’t ask to be supported. Connect with the person instead.3. Support people “at your level”. If you have 1,000 followers, find others who do.4. If someone never supported you, or clearly stopped, consider pausing it too.

DO’s of supporting each other:

- Leaving long comments (15+ words). Just like a normal post (with a hook).- Join groups to support each other. On my Slack, people leave a link to their Linkedin posts, and hope some people will connect with it. This is healthy.

DON’Ts of supporting each other:

- Never use automation and AI-generated comments. It will block your account, or worse, destroy its reach because of a shadow ban.- Never use pods. Pods are places where you post a link and expect bots to comment on it. You will attract bots, and Linkedin will know it.- Never share a link with the UTM. When sharing a Linkedin post URL, you must remove what is after the ‘?’.

Now, before you jump on the occasion to ask me to comment on your posts, ask yourself this question: **how many people ask me? what’s in it for me?**

And that’s the deepest layer of the social media game theory.

✦ If you keep asking without ever giving, you won’t receive anything.

✦ If you keep giving **exactly what the other person would love to receive**, you’ll keep receiving yourself as well.

And what I bolded is the most crucial part: it’s on you to understand and determine what is the junction between what you can offer (from you/your network) and what is the other person’s goals.

#### B. One format is better than every other combined.

Single image rules them all.

Not a video. Not text. Not polls.

A single image, formatted as a 1080 x 1350 (**4:5 HD**) image.

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/b8f0e34f-cc85-422e-adfa-adac5dc9d819_1024x475.png)

But not any kind of image:

- Never be funny. Funny does not work on Linkedin.- Never be inspirational. You will most likely fail at it.- Never brand it. No one cares about your name & colors.- Never be personal. Selfies do not get you new followers (they don’t know you).

Instead, you must:

- Be educational. Teach us one simple thing to be better at [X].- Reduce confusion. I must print your image & explain to you what’s going on.- Inspire someone to ACT on something they must do/ not do to be better at [X].

Maria Zhanette casually got 1,000+ followers per single image.

→ So mastering single images is (1) faster (2) easier (3) achievable.

✦ *How can you replicate it if you’re terrible at design?*

It’s all about **repurposing***.

> repurposing is reusing a past viral post, again.usually its format, its angle, its hook…

You only need 5 super viral themes, hooks, designs, angles…

You can find them online (Pinterest, Instagram, X) from others, or from your own posts. And then you use AI (or your two hands, or the ones from your designer) to recreate them.

✦ Start by making 10 completely different designs, hooks, angles.

✦ Pick the winners, repurpose them by changing one variable (of the 3).

✦ You will end up knowing what the winning combination *(for you)* is.

✦ Double down on it. Ad vitam eternam (*it means forever, but cooler*).

## 3. You do it for the wrong reasons.

I gave you the exact playbook to get 10,000 followers.

And it works.

If I hired a new employee today, I’d grow her account from 0 → 10,000 followers in less than a month. Every single time.

But the truth is, you want 10,000 followers for the wrong reasons.

#### A. 10,000 followers is an ego trip.

You want the followers of a content creator, but you don’t want to be a content creator. Stop trying to be me, Ruben Hassid. This is a full-time job.

This is the same difference as:

✦ You want to be healthy. Lifting weights *(you should)*.

✦ And you also want the body of a bodybuilder.

But the bodybuilder has a life you would never want to have.

You don’t want to eat this much every day.

You don’t want to stop alcohol & parties.

You don’t want to spend hours every day.

You don’t want to sleep at 9 pm.

So stop wanting a bodybuilder's body. Stop wanting 10,000 (or a million) followers.

#### B. This guy has 3,000 followers and makes more than I do.

I know someone who’s into real estate.

He has 3,000 followers - and he does not post in English.

And yet, his Linkedin generates more business than I do.

That’s because he is playing a different game than I am.

A game that I think 99% of you reading this right now should play.

✦ Be **consistent**. To be known as the [X] guy.

✦ Sell **high-ticket** services/products. So any inbound DMs are a massive win.

✦ Optimize everything to get **DMs**. Your content should spark conversations.

So, aiming at 10,000 followers was wrong in the first place.

You want the wrong thing.

## 4. A braindump of my stupid rules to follow.

These rules fit no section, and I will keep updating them:

☑ Location **matters**. If you are in a developed country, it will be harder to grow.

☑ Never use the green tag “**Open to work**”. It’s a red flag.

☑ Go to your settings, and make sure everything is **public**.

☑ Verify your account with ID, don’t change your name

☑ One **language** only on your profile and your posts. I suggest English only. Unless you are 100% confident that nearly all of your clients speak one language.

☑ Don’t open more than 3 Linkedin **tabs** on your computer. Or you’ll get restricted. I know you want to, but just don’t do it.

☑ Don’t use VPN. It’s sketchy. Use an** ISP** (e.g., Bright Data) = residential **proxy**. That’s the only way to get multiple people on your account without risking it.

☑ Don’t use an AI browser agent or **automation**. You are killing your account.

☑ Make your button “**follow me**” first instead of “connect with me”.
