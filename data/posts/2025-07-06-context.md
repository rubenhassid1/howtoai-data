You obsess over little details of your ChatGPT prompts:

1. Which model should you use?2. What “role” should ChatGPT have?3. What “steps” should ChatGPT follow?

The right prompts do bring better outputs.

But the most important part of the prompt is the right **context**.

☑ **Who** the answer is for and **Why** they should care.

☑ **What** facts, examples, or links the AI must know (and what to avoid).

☑**How** success will look; format, tone, length, next‑step workflow.

I call it the **4W** prompt for better context engineering.

Think of ChatGPT like a very good intern: it does exceptionally well… if it knows first the 4W to perform the tasks. And I’m not the only one obsessing over it:

## #1 - How to use the 4W prompt.

The common misconception I keep hearing is “*ChatGPT knows everything on Google anyway, so why should I care sharing context?*”.

Well, it does not.

Google has 50 billion webpages. ChatGPT can’t ‘*know*’ the entire web.

Here’s how to (really) prompt it instead.

Let’s say you want to ask something to ChatGPT beyond a Google search.

For example:

1. Summarize a 100-page PDF, find gaps & search the web to complete it.2. Share 10 novel ideas to double-down on your past marketing campaign.3. Write a draft of a presentation inspired by a TED talk you love.

You need the right context. So I made a prompt to copy & paste called **4W**.

You must cover **Who, Why, What, How** when starting your chat.

Copy & paste this [prompt](https://docs.google.com/document/d/18DjZ4AY8LXDCbHVsCM9Jr1lFCbnYikDnSM3vCR2F4ZE/edit?usp=sharing) (with instructions):

[paste or summarise source]  2. [add links if ChatGPT does not block it]  3. ### Example 1: From a long PDF to a one-pager.

A friend of mine is a Private Equity Analyst, and ask me for a good prompt.

So let’s roleplay as him:

☑* Who are you?* A private equity analyst.

☑* Why do you use ChatGPT?* To summarize a 92-page PDF into one page.

☑* What do you need from ChatGPT?* Extract key data for my specific boss questions.

☑* How do you need ChatGPT to reason?* Following a strict company methodology.

I attached this (massive) [92-page PDF](https://www.amf-france.org/sites/institutionnel/files/private/2023-09/230911-private-equity-overview-and-vulnerabilities-l.-grillet-aubert-en.pdf) about Private Equity.

Time to fill out my 4W prompt:

Insert headline quantitative benchmarks from the PDF2. Insert qualitative findings that illuminate opportunities or risks3. Gap check – list any information still missing; ask me concise questions until gaps are filled.  Step 2. Plan – outline a logical structure or bullet agenda for the piece. Wait for my approval.Step 3. Draft – write the first version following the approved plan.  Step 4. Review – pause and ask me for feedback on clarity, tone and completeness.  Step 5. Revise – improve the draft with my notes.  Repeat steps 3‑4 until I agree with the command AGREE.CONTEXT-HANDLING RULES:If a pasted source exceeds \~200 words, first give me a one-sentence summary and ask whether to keep the full text. ## #3 - When not to share context (& what to do instead).

Context Is All You Need.

1. It limits hallucinations.2. It gives you the right answer.3. It gives it to you the right way.

But a lot of the time, you need a quick answer.

- Quick text fixes (spelling, grammar, re-formatting, translation).- Simple mathematics or conversions (°F → °C, 5 MB → KB).- Small data grabs or labels (pull an email, tag sentiment).- Basic fact look-ups (“Symbol for gold?”).

Here’s my simple rule:

→ **I need reasoning**: 4W Prompt + Deep Research (in Tools).

→ **I need quick or no context**: Normal Prompt + GPT-4.5 model.
